# Phishing and Social Engineering

## What I Learned

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers attempt to trick people into revealing sensitive information or performing unsafe actions.

Social engineering refers more broadly to manipulating human behavior to gain access, information, or trust.

Common phishing examples include:

- fake login pages
- suspicious email attachments
- urgent password reset requests
- fake invoices or delivery notifications

## Why It Matters

One thing I found interesting is that phishing attacks often target people and workflows rather than only technical systems.

This means cybersecurity is also connected to:

- communication
- awareness
- process design
- organizational culture

Even strong technical systems may still be vulnerable if users are pressured into clicking malicious links or sharing sensitive information.

## AI and Phishing

Generative AI may make phishing attempts more convincing by producing:

- more natural language
- personalized messages
- realistic formatting
- multilingual communication

This increases the importance of:

- critical thinking
- verification processes
- security awareness

## Example

A phishing email may appear to come from an internal IT department asking employees to urgently reset their password through a fake link.

Because the message looks professional and creates urgency, users may trust it without carefully verifying the source.

## Personal Observation

I also noticed how phishing attempts can closely imitate normal daily workflows.

For example, while waiting for online shopping deliveries, I sometimes received text messages pretending to be from delivery carriers such as DAO. The messages claimed there was a problem with the parcel delivery and asked me to click a link to update information.

At first glance, the messages looked believable because:

- I was already expecting a package,
- the carrier name felt familiar,
- and the message created urgency.

However, the website address was suspicious and did not match the official carrier domain.

This made me realize how phishing attacks often rely on timing, familiarity, and human behavior rather than purely technical methods.

## Reflection

This topic helped me realize that cybersecurity is not only about technical protection, but also about understanding human behavior, communication patterns, and operational processes.

It also showed me why security awareness and clear workflows are important in organizations using digital tools and AI systems.
