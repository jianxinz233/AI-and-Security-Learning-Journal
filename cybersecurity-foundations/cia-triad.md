# CIA Triad

## What I Learned

The CIA triad is a foundational cybersecurity model built around three core principles:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

These principles help organizations think about how systems and information should be protected.

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality means protecting information from unauthorized access.

Examples:

- customer personal data
- passwords
- financial records

Organizations often use:

- authentication
- access control
- encryption

to support confidentiality.

## Integrity

Integrity means keeping information accurate, complete, and trustworthy.

Examples:

- preventing unauthorized data modification
- ensuring transaction records remain accurate
- protecting systems from tampering

## Availability

Availability means ensuring systems and information are accessible when needed.

Examples:

- online banking systems
- customer support platforms
- internal company systems

Downtime, system failures, or cyberattacks may affect availability.

## Business Perspective

I found it interesting that the CIA triad is not only a technical concept, but also connected to trust, operations, and customer experience.

For example, a company may have strong functionality, but if users cannot trust the security or reliability of the system, adoption and business value may be affected.

## Reflection

Learning about the CIA triad helped me better understand that cybersecurity is closely connected to business continuity, communication, and organizational responsibility, not only technical protection.
